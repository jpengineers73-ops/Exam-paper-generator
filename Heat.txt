What is the degree of hotness or coldness of an object called? | Temperature
Which device is used to measure body temperature? | Clinical Thermometer
What is the normal temperature of the human body in Celsius? | 37 Degrees C
What is the range of a laboratory thermometer? | -10C to 110C
Name the process by which heat is transferred in solids. | Conduction
Name the process by which heat is transferred in liquids and gases. | Convection
How does heat from the Sun reach the Earth? | Radiation
Which materials allow heat to pass through them easily? | Conductors
Materials that do not allow heat to pass through are called? | Insulators (or poor conductors)
What is the range of a clinical thermometer? | 35C to 42C
Why is the box of a solar cooker painted black from inside? | To absorb more heat
In which direction does heat always flow? | From a hotter object to a colder object
What is the liquid used in older thermometers? | Mercury
The breeze blowing from the sea towards the land during the day is? | Sea Breeze
The breeze blowing from the land towards the sea at night is? | Land Breeze
Which type of clothes are preferred during winters? | Woolen clothes
Why do we wear light-colored clothes in summer? | They reflect most of the heat
What prevents the mercury level from falling on its own in a clinical thermometer? | Kink (or constriction)
Does radiation require a medium for heat transfer? | No
Which is a better conductor of heat: Iron or Wood? | Iron
Stainless steel pans are usually provided with copper bottoms because? | Copper is a better conductor than steel
Air is a _____ conductor of heat. | Poor (or Insulator)
What happens to a substance when it is heated? | It expands
Which process of heat transfer is responsible for the formation of wind? | Convection
Why are woolen clothes warm? | Air is trapped between wool fibers, which is a poor conductor of heat
